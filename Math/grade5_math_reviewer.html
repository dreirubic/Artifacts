Topic 01

Basic Geometric Ideas

Key Concepts
Point
An exact location in space. It has no size, length, or shape.
• Point A
Line
A straight path that goes on forever in BOTH directions. No endpoints.
↔ Line AB
Line Segment
A part of a line with TWO endpoints. It has a definite length.
|–––| Segment AB
Ray
A part of a line with ONE endpoint. It goes on forever in one direction.
•→ Ray AB
Plane
A flat surface that extends forever in all directions. It has no thickness.
Like a floor going on forever
Parallel Lines
Lines that never meet. They stay the same distance apart forever.
= Train rails, notebook lines
Perpendicular Lines
Lines that intersect and form a 90° (right) angle.
+ like a plus sign or a cross
Intersecting Lines
Lines that cross each other at exactly one point.
× like an X shape
🌟 Remember! Point → no dimensions | Line Segment → 2 endpoints | Ray → 1 endpoint | Line → 0 endpoints. Parallel lines NEVER meet. Perpendicular lines always make a 90° corner!
Practice Questions
1
What geometric figure has NO endpoints and extends forever in both directions?
▼
Answer
A Line
A line has no endpoints — it goes on forever in both directions. Unlike a ray (1 endpoint) or segment (2 endpoints).
2
Train tracks running side by side without ever meeting are an example of _______ lines.
▼
Answer
Parallel lines
Parallel lines never intersect and always remain the same distance apart — just like train tracks!
3
The corner of your notebook where two sides meet is an example of _______ lines.
▼
Answer
Perpendicular lines
Perpendicular lines meet at a right angle (90°), just like the corners of a notebook or a square.
4
Name the geometric figure: it starts at point A and goes on forever toward point B, but does NOT go back past A.
▼
Answer
A Ray (Ray AB)
A ray has exactly one endpoint (point A) and extends infinitely in one direction only.

Topic 02

Angles

Key Concepts

An angle is formed by two rays that share a common endpoint called the vertex. We measure angles in degrees (°).

⊾
Acute Angle
Less than 90°
Sharp and pointy
∟
Right Angle
Exactly 90°
Has a small square corner
∠
Obtuse Angle
90° to 180°
Wide and open
↔
Straight Angle
Exactly 180°
Looks like a line
↺
Reflex Angle
180° to 360°
Bends more than halfway
🌟 Easy Trick to Remember! "A-cute" angle is cute and small (less than 90°). "Ob-tuse" means dull/blunt — it's wide (between 90° and 180°). A RIGHT angle has a RIGHT-angle square marker. A STRAIGHT angle is a straight line = 180°.
Practice Questions
1
What type of angle measures exactly 90°?
▼
Answer
Right Angle
A right angle measures exactly 90°. It looks like the corner of a square and is marked with a small square symbol.
2
An angle that measures 135° is a/an _______ angle.
▼
Answer
Obtuse angle
135° is between 90° and 180°, so it is an obtuse angle. It is wider than a right angle but hasn't reached a straight line yet.
3
Classify the angle: the tip of a slice of pizza measures about 30°.
▼
Answer
Acute angle
30° is less than 90°, making it an acute angle — sharp and small, just like the pointy tip of a pizza slice!
4
What do you call the common endpoint of the two rays that form an angle?
▼
Answer
The Vertex
The vertex is the corner point of the angle — where the two rays meet.
5
What is the sum of angles in a straight angle?
▼
Answer
180°
A straight angle looks like a flat, straight line. It measures exactly 180°.

Topic 03

Polygons

Key Concepts

A polygon is a closed, flat shape made of straight line segments. They are named by their number of sides.

Triangle
3 sides, 3 angles
Sum of angles = 180°
Quadrilateral
4 sides, 4 angles
Sum of angles = 360°
Pentagon
5 sides, 5 angles
Like the Pentagon building
Hexagon
6 sides, 6 angles
Honeybee cells!
Heptagon
7 sides, 7 angles
Hepta = 7
Octagon
8 sides, 8 angles
Like a STOP sign!
Types of Quadrilaterals:
Square
4 equal sides, 4 right angles
Rectangle
Opposite sides equal, 4 right angles
Rhombus
4 equal sides, opposite angles equal
Trapezoid
Exactly ONE pair of parallel sides
Parallelogram
Two pairs of parallel sides
🌟 Regular vs Irregular Polygon A REGULAR polygon has ALL sides equal AND all angles equal (e.g., a regular hexagon). An IRREGULAR polygon does not — sides and angles can be different sizes.
Practice Questions
1
What is the sum of the interior angles of any triangle?
▼
Answer
180°
No matter what kind of triangle — small, big, thin, wide — the three angles always add up to 180°.
2
A STOP sign has 8 sides. What polygon is it?
▼
Answer
Octagon
Oct = 8. An octagon has 8 sides and 8 angles. STOP signs are octagons!
3
Which quadrilateral has EXACTLY one pair of parallel sides?
▼
Answer
Trapezoid
A trapezoid has only one pair of parallel sides. (A parallelogram, rectangle, rhombus, and square all have TWO pairs.)
4
What do you call a polygon where ALL sides are equal AND all angles are equal?
▼
Answer
Regular Polygon
A regular polygon is perfectly even — every side is the same length, and every angle is the same measure. Example: a regular hexagon or a square.
5
Name the quadrilateral: 4 equal sides, opposite sides are parallel, but the angles are NOT 90°.
▼
Answer
Rhombus
A rhombus has 4 equal sides like a square, but its angles are not right angles — it looks like a "pushed-over" square (like a diamond shape).

Topic 04

Circle

Key Concepts

A circle is a closed curve where every point is the same distance from the center.

Center
The middle point of the circle. All edges are equal distance from here.
Radius (r)
Distance from the center to any point on the circle.
r = ½ × diameter
Diameter (d)
Distance across the circle through the center. Always passes through the middle.
d = 2 × radius
Chord
A line segment connecting any two points on the circle. (Diameter is the longest chord.)
Arc
A curved part of the circle's edge (circumference).
Like a curved road segment
Circumference
The total distance around the circle (its perimeter).
C = π × d ≈ 3.14 × d
d = 2r Diameter = 2 × radius
r = d ÷ 2 Radius = half of diameter
C = πd Circumference (π ≈ 3.14)
🌟 Remember! The RADIUS goes from the CENTER to the edge. The DIAMETER goes ALL THE WAY across (through the center). Diameter = 2 × Radius. The CHORD connects two points on the circle but may NOT pass through the center.
Practice Questions
1
A circle has a radius of 7 cm. What is its diameter?
▼
Answer
14 cm
Diameter = 2 × radius = 2 × 7 = 14 cm.
2
The diameter of a circle is 20 cm. What is the radius?
▼
Answer
10 cm
Radius = Diameter ÷ 2 = 20 ÷ 2 = 10 cm.
3
What is the name of the line segment connecting TWO points on a circle that passes through the CENTER?
▼
Answer
Diameter
The diameter is the longest chord of a circle and always passes through the center.
4
What is a curved part of the circumference (outer edge) of a circle called?
▼
Answer
Arc
An arc is any portion of the circumference — the curved outer edge of a circle.

Topic 05

Solid Figures

Key Concepts

Solid figures are 3D shapes. They have faces (flat surfaces), edges (where faces meet), and vertices (corner points).

Cube
6 square faces, 12 edges, 8 vertices. All sides equal.
🎲 Dice, ice cube
Rectangular Prism
6 rectangular faces, 12 edges, 8 vertices.
📦 Cereal box, book
Cylinder
2 circular faces, 1 curved surface, 0 vertices.
🥫 Can, drum
Cone
1 circular base, 1 curved surface, 1 vertex (apex).
🍦 Ice cream cone, party hat
Sphere
1 curved surface, 0 faces, 0 edges, 0 vertices.
🏀 Ball, Earth, bubble
Pyramid
Triangular faces meeting at one apex. Base can be any polygon.
🔺 Egyptian pyramids
🌟 Euler's Formula (for polyhedra) Faces + Vertices − Edges = 2. Example for a cube: 6 + 8 − 12 = 2 ✓. A face is a FLAT surface. An edge is where TWO faces meet. A vertex is where THREE or more edges meet (a corner).
Practice Questions
1
How many faces, edges, and vertices does a cube have?
▼
Answer
6 faces, 12 edges, 8 vertices
A cube has 6 square faces (like the 6 sides of a dice), 12 edges (where faces meet), and 8 vertices (corners).
2
Which solid figure has NO flat faces and NO edges?
▼
Answer
Sphere
A sphere is perfectly round with only one curved surface — no flat faces, no edges, and no vertices.
3
An ice cream cone is shaped like what solid figure?
▼
Answer
Cone
A cone has a circular base, a curved surface, and comes to a single point called the apex — just like an ice cream cone!
4
What is the difference between a cube and a rectangular prism?
▼
Answer
A cube has ALL equal square faces. A rectangular prism has rectangular faces that may have different lengths.
A cube is a special rectangular prism where all 6 faces are identical squares. A rectangular prism (like a shoebox) can have different lengths, widths, and heights.
5
How many edges does a rectangular prism have?
▼
Answer
12 edges
Like a cube, a rectangular prism also has 12 edges: 4 on the top, 4 on the bottom, and 4 vertical edges connecting them.

Topic 06

Temperature

Key Concepts

Temperature measures how hot or cold something is. We use Celsius (°C) and Fahrenheit (°F).

🧊
Freezing Point
0°C / 32°F
Water turns to ice
🌤️
Room Temperature
About 25°C
Comfortable & normal
🔥
Boiling Point
100°C / 212°F
Water turns to steam
🤒
Body Temperature
About 37°C
Normal human body
°F to °C: (°F − 32) × 5/9
°C to °F: (°C × 9/5) + 32
🌟 Remember! If temperature RISES → ADD. If temperature DROPS/FALLS → SUBTRACT. If it goes BELOW ZERO → use negative numbers (e.g., −5°C means 5 degrees below freezing).
Practice Questions
1
At what temperature (°C) does water freeze?
▼
Answer
0°C
Water freezes (turns into ice) at 0°C. This is called the freezing point of water.
2
The temperature in the morning is 18°C. By afternoon, it rose by 9°C. What is the afternoon temperature?
▼
Answer
27°C
Temperature rose (increased), so we ADD: 18 + 9 = 27°C.
3
It was 5°C outside. The temperature dropped 8°C overnight. What is the new temperature?
▼
Answer
−3°C
Temperature dropped (decreased), so we SUBTRACT: 5 − 8 = −3°C (3 degrees below zero).
4
What is the boiling point of water in Celsius?
▼
Answer
100°C
Water boils (turns to steam) at 100°C, which is the same as 212°F.

AM vs PM
AM = midnight to noon (12:00 AM to 11:59 AM). PM = noon to midnight (12:00 PM to 11:59 PM).
Elapsed Time
The amount of time that has passed between a start time and an end time.
End time − Start time
🌟 How to Find Elapsed Time Step 1: Start with the start time. Step 2: Add hours first, then minutes. Step 3: If minutes go over 60, carry over to the hours! Example: 2:40 PM + 1 hr 30 min → 2:40 + 1hr = 3:40 → 3:40 + 30 min = 4:10 PM.

From 8:45 to 9:45 = 1 hour. From 9:45 to 10:15 = 30 minutes. Total = 1 hr 30 min.

Topic 08

Number Patterns & Sequences

Key Concepts

A number pattern is a sequence of numbers that follows a rule. Finding the rule lets us predict the next numbers!

Arithmetic Sequence
Add or subtract the SAME number each time.
2, 5, 8, 11, 14... (+3 each time)
Geometric Sequence
Multiply or divide by the SAME number each time.
3, 6, 12, 24... (×2 each time)
Square Numbers
Numbers multiplied by themselves.
1, 4, 9, 16, 25, 36... (1², 2², 3²...)
Odd Numbers
Numbers that cannot be divided evenly by 2.
1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11...
Even Numbers
Numbers divisible evenly by 2.
2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12...
Fibonacci-like
Add the two previous numbers to get the next.
1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13...
🌟 How to Find the Rule Look at what changes between numbers: Are you ADDING or SUBTRACTING the same amount? → Arithmetic. Are you MULTIPLYING or DIVIDING? → Geometric. Once you find the rule, apply it to find missing terms!
Practice Questions
1
Find the next two numbers: 4, 8, 12, 16, ___, ___
▼
Answer
20, 24
The rule is +4 each time (arithmetic sequence). 16 + 4 = 20. 20 + 4 = 24.
2
What is the rule for this pattern? 2, 6, 18, 54, 162
▼
Answer
Multiply by 3 each time (×3)
2×3=6, 6×3=18, 18×3=54, 54×3=162. This is a geometric sequence with a common ratio of 3.
3
Find the missing number: 5, 10, ___, 20, 25
▼
Answer
15
The rule is +5 each time. 10 + 5 = 15. Check: 15 + 5 = 20 ✓
4
What are the first 5 square numbers?
▼
Answer
1, 4, 9, 16, 25
1²=1, 2²=4, 3²=9, 4²=16, 5²=25. Square numbers are made by multiplying a number by itself.
5
What comes next? 100, 90, 80, 70, ___
▼
Answer
60
The rule is −10 each time. 70 − 10 = 60.

Topic 09

Solving Equations

Key Concepts

An equation is a mathematical statement that two expressions are EQUAL (using the = sign). We solve for the unknown variable (usually n, x, or y).

Variable
A letter (like x, n, y) that represents an unknown number we need to find.
n + 5 = 12 → n = ?
Inverse Operations
Opposite operations used to isolate the variable: + and − are opposites. × and ÷ are opposites.
Balance Rule
Whatever you do to ONE side, you MUST do to the OTHER side to keep the equation balanced.
Step-by-Step Examples
Addition equation: x + 8 = 15 → x = 15 − 8 → x = 7
Subtraction equation: y − 6 = 9 → y = 9 + 6 → y = 15
Multiplication equation: 5n = 35 → n = 35 ÷ 5 → n = 7
Division equation: n ÷ 4 = 3 → n = 3 × 4 → n = 12
🌟 The Golden Rule To solve for a variable, use the OPPOSITE (inverse) operation. Addition ↔ Subtraction. Multiplication ↔ Division. Always do the SAME thing to BOTH sides of the equation!
Practice Questions
1
Solve: n + 14 = 25
▼
Answer
n = 11
n + 14 = 25 → n = 25 − 14 → n = 11. Check: 11 + 14 = 25 ✓
2
Solve: 6x = 42
▼
Answer
x = 7
6x = 42 → x = 42 ÷ 6 → x = 7. Check: 6 × 7 = 42 ✓
3
Solve: y − 11 = 8
▼
Answer
y = 19
y − 11 = 8 → y = 8 + 11 → y = 19. Check: 19 − 11 = 8 ✓
4
Solve: n ÷ 5 = 9
▼
Answer
n = 45
n ÷ 5 = 9 → n = 9 × 5 → n = 45. Check: 45 ÷ 5 = 9 ✓
5
Yumi has some stickers. She gives away 13 and has 27 left. Write and solve an equation to find how many she started with.
▼
Answer
n − 13 = 27 → n = 40 stickers
Let n = the starting number of stickers. n − 13 = 27. n = 27 + 13 = 40. Yumi started with 40 stickers.

Topic 10

Line Graphs

Key Concepts

A line graph uses points connected by lines to show how data changes over time.

X-axis (Horizontal)
Usually shows TIME or categories (days, months, years).
Y-axis (Vertical)
Usually shows the DATA VALUE being measured (temperature, score, sales).
Data Points
Dots placed where the x and y values meet. Connected by a line.
Title
Tells us what the graph is about. Always written at the top.
Scale
The numbers on the axes that show the range and intervals of data.
Trend
Whether the data is going UP (increasing), going DOWN (decreasing), or staying the SAME.
🌟 Reading a Line Graph 1. Read the TITLE first. 2. Look at both axis LABELS to understand what is being measured. 3. Find a point and read across to the y-axis for the value. 4. Look for TRENDS — is the line going up, down, or flat? Line graphs are best for showing CHANGE OVER TIME!
Practice Questions
1
What is a line graph best used to show?
▼
Answer
Changes in data over time
Line graphs excel at showing trends over time — like temperature changes during a week, or test scores across months.
2
On a line graph showing daily temperature, what would you usually find on the X-axis (horizontal)?
▼
Answer
The days (time / categories)
The X-axis (horizontal) usually shows TIME — days, months, or years. The Y-axis shows the data values, like temperature in °C.
3
A line graph shows these temperatures: Mon=30°C, Tue=32°C, Wed=29°C, Thu=31°C. On which day was it hottest?
▼
Answer
Tuesday (32°C)
Looking at the data points, Tuesday had the highest temperature at 32°C — this would be the highest point on the line graph.
4
If a line graph shows a line going steadily UPWARD from left to right, what does this mean?
▼
Answer
The data is increasing (going up) over time.
A rising line = increasing trend. A falling line = decreasing trend. A flat line = no change. Reading the trend is one of the most important skills in reading line graphs.

Topic 11

Probability

Key Concepts

Probability is the chance that something will happen. It is expressed as a fraction, decimal, or percent between 0 and 1.

🚫
Impossible
Probability = 0
Will NEVER happen
🎲
Unlikely
Less than 1/2
Probably won't happen
⚖️
Equally Likely
Exactly 1/2
50/50 chance
✅
Likely
More than 1/2
Probably will happen
💯
Certain
Probability = 1
Will ALWAYS happen
P(event) = Favorable outcomes ÷ Total outcomes
Examples
🪙 Flipping a coin → P(heads) = 1/2 (1 head out of 2 sides)
🎲 Rolling a die → P(getting a 3) = 1/6 (one 3 out of six faces)
🔴 Bag: 4 red, 6 blue → P(red) = 4/10 = 2/5
🟢 Bag: 4 red, 6 blue → P(blue) = 6/10 = 3/5
🌟 Remember! Probability is ALWAYS between 0 and 1 (or 0% and 100%). P = 0 means IMPOSSIBLE. P = 1 means CERTAIN. The bigger the fraction, the MORE likely the event. All probabilities of all outcomes must ADD UP to 1!
Practice Questions
1
A bag has 5 red marbles and 5 blue marbles. What is the probability of picking a red marble?
▼
Answer
5/10 = 1/2
P(red) = 5 red ÷ 10 total = 5/10 = 1/2. There's an equal chance (50-50) of picking either color.
2
A jar has 2 green, 4 yellow, and 4 orange candies. What is the probability of picking a yellow candy?
▼
Answer
4/10 = 2/5
Total candies = 2 + 4 + 4 = 10. Yellow candies = 4. P(yellow) = 4/10 = 2/5.
3
You roll a standard die (numbered 1–6). What is the probability of rolling a number GREATER than 4?
▼
Answer
2/6 = 1/3
Numbers greater than 4 are: 5 and 6 — that's 2 favorable outcomes out of 6 total. P = 2/6 = 1/3.
4
What probability word describes an event that will DEFINITELY happen (probability = 1)?
▼
Answer
Certain
A certain event always happens — its probability is 1 (or 100%). Example: "The sun will rise tomorrow."
5
A spinner has 8 equal sections: 3 red, 3 blue, 2 green. What color are you MOST likely to land on?
▼
Answer
Either Red or Blue (equally most likely)
P(red) = 3/8, P(blue) = 3/8, P(green) = 2/8. Red and blue are tied as the most likely outcomes since they both have the same probability (3/8 each).
