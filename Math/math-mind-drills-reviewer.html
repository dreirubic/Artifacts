⚡ Key Formulas — Know These by Heart!

🟦
Area of a Rectangle
Area = Length × Width
A = L × W
Answer is always in square units (m², cm²)
⬛
Area of a Square
Area = Side × Side
A = s²
All sides of a square are equal!
📏
Perimeter of a Rectangle
P = 2 × (L + W)
or P = 2L + 2W
Add all four sides!
🔷
Perimeter of a Square
P = 4 × Side
P = 4s
Multiply one side by 4!
📦
Volume of a Box
V = L × W × H
Answer is in cubic units (cm³, m³). Multiply length, width, AND height!
🔺
Perimeter of a Triangle
P = s + s + s
Add all three sides! For equilateral: P = 3 × side

🟩 Area

Area measures how much surface is covered. Think of it as how many square tiles you need to fill a floor!
Example 1: A grass lawn is 85 m long and 68 m wide. What is its area?
→ A = L × W = 85 × 68 = 5,780 m²

Example 2: A lobby is 16 m by 8 m. What is its area?
→ A = 16 × 8 = 128 m²

Example 3: A rectangular garden has an area of 162 m² and width of 9 m. Find the length.
→ Length = Area ÷ Width = 162 ÷ 9 = 18 m

Example 4 (Square): A square has area of 36 m². Its side is doubled. Find new area.
→ Original side = √36 = 6 m → Doubled side = 12 m → New area = 12 × 12 = 144 m²

📐 Perimeter

Perimeter is the total distance around a shape. Think of it as a fence going all the way around!
Example 1: A town plaza is 56 m long and 48 m wide. A jogger goes around it 10 times. How many km?
→ Perimeter = 2 × (56 + 48) = 2 × 104 = 208 m
→ 10 rounds = 208 × 10 = 2,080 m = 2.08 km

Example 2: Lito has a garden 15 m long and 9 m wide. Perimeter?
→ P = 2 × (15 + 9) = 2 × 24 = 48 m

Example 3: Jack has a garden 12 m long and 9 m wide. Fencing costs ₱37.50/m. Total cost?
→ P = 2 × (12 + 9) = 42 m → Cost = 42 × 37.50 = ₱1,575

Example 4: Door is 120 cm wide. Height = 120 + 80 = 200 cm. Perimeter?
→ P = 2 × (120 + 200) = 2 × 320 = 640 cm

Example 5: Flower garden is 12 m wide. Length = 12 + 3 = 15 m. Perimeter?
→ P = 2 × (12 + 15) = 2 × 27 = 54 m

📦 Volume

Volume measures how much space is inside a 3D shape. Think of it as how many cubes can fit inside a box!
Example: A box is 14 cm long, 11 cm wide, and 9 cm high. What is its volume?
→ V = L × W × H = 14 × 11 × 9 = 1,386 cm³

💡 Word Problem Tips

🔍
READ carefully
What is being asked? Perimeter, area, or volume? Look for keywords!
✏️
KEYWORDS
"Around" = Perimeter 📏
"Surface / Cover" = Area 🟦
"Inside / Capacity" = Volume 📦
🔄
CONVERT FIRST
Always make sure units match BEFORE calculating! 1 m = 100 cm, 1 km = 1,000 m
✅
CHECK YOUR UNITS
Length → m, cm
Area → m², cm²
Volume → m³, cm³

✂️ Cutting / Pieces Problems

To find how many pieces can be cut: Total length ÷ Size of each piece
Example 1: A ribbon is 60 dm long. How many 2-dm pieces can be cut?
→ 60 ÷ 2 = 30 pieces

Example 2: From 30 m of ribbon, how many 4-dm pieces?
→ Convert: 30 m = 300 dm → 300 ÷ 4 = 75 pieces

Example 3: From a piece of cardolina 8 cm by 6 cm, how many cards 8 cm × 3 dm?
→ Convert 3 dm = 30 cm. Area of cardolina = 8×6 = 48 cm². Area of card = 8×30 = 240 cm².
→ Wait — check units! Cards per sheet = 6 ÷ 3 = 2 (per row). Think in strips!
