🧩 What is a Multi-step Problem?

A multi-step problem needs more than one math step to find the answer. You have to solve it one step at a time!

💡 Think of it like climbing stairs — you go up one step, then the next!

🪜 How to Solve It (4 Steps!)

1

UNDERSTAND — Read the problem carefully. What is being asked? What do you know?

2

PLAN — What operations will you use? (+, −, ×, ÷) How many steps do you need?

3

SOLVE — Do the math! Solve Step 1 first, then use that answer for Step 2.

4

CHECK — Does your answer make sense? Go back and review!

✏️ Let's Try an Example!

📖 Word Problem:

Ana has 8 apples. She gave 3 to her friend. Then she bought 5 more. How many apples does Ana have now?

✅ Step 1:

8 − 3 = 5 apples left after giving some away

✅ Step 2:

5 + 5 = 10 apples in all!

🌟

Ana has 10 apples now! Always write your final answer clearly.

📝 Key Words to Watch For

in all / altogether → Add ➕ left / remaining → Subtract ➖ each / groups of → Multiply ✖️ shared equally → Divide ➗

Sometimes a problem has two of these words — that means two steps! 👀

🍕 What is a Fraction?

A fraction shows a part of a whole. When we cut something into equal pieces and take some of them, we use a fraction!

🍕 Imagine cutting a pizza into equal slices and eating some!

🌟 Fractional Part of a Set

A set is a group of objects! A fraction can also describe part of a group — not just one object.

🍎 Example: 2 out of 5 apples are red → 2/5 of the set are red apples!

👉 How to Find Fractional Part of a Set

1

Count the total number of objects in the set. This is your Denominator.

2

Count the objects that match what the problem is asking. This is your Numerator.

3

Write the fraction: matching on top, total on bottom!

🌈 Let's Try!

📖 Example:

There are 8 balloons. 3 are yellow. What fraction of the balloons are yellow?

🟡 🟡 🟡 🔴 🔴 🔵 🔵 🟢
✅ Answer:

Yellow balloons = 3 → Numerator = 3

Total balloons = 8 → Denominator = 8

So: 3/8 of the balloons are yellow! 🟡

📖 Another Example:

There are 10 stars. 6 are gold. What fraction are gold?

⭐⭐⭐ ⭐⭐⭐ 🌟🌟🌟🌟
✅ Answer: 6/10 of the stars are gold! ⭐

🧮 Finding a Fractional Part (How Many?)

Sometimes the problem gives you the fraction and total, and asks "how many?"

1

Divide the total by the denominator to find one part.

2

Multiply that by the numerator to get your answer!

📖 Example: What is 3/4 of 8?

Step 1: 8 ÷ 4 = 2 (one part)

Step 2: 2 × 3 = 6

3/4 of 8 = 6! 🎉

🍰 Similar Fractions (Same Denominator)

Similar fractions have the same denominator (same bottom number)!

👉 When the denominator is the same — just compare the numerators! Bigger numerator = bigger fraction!

Which is bigger: 1/5 or 3/5?

1/5
3/5
✅ Answer:

3 > 1, so 3/5 > 1/5! The orange bar is longer! 🟠

🍫 Dissimilar Fractions (Different Denominator)

Dissimilar fractions have different denominators!

👉 Use drawings or fraction bars to compare! Bigger piece of the same whole = bigger fraction.

Which is bigger: 1/2 or 1/4?

1/2
1/4
✅ Answer:

1/2 > 1/4 because 1/2 takes up more space! Pink bar is longer! 🩷

📋 Ordering Fractions

We can also put fractions in order from smallest to biggest (Ascending) or biggest to smallest (Descending)!

📖 Example — Order from smallest to biggest:

3/8   1/8   5/8   2/8

Same denominator → compare numerators: 1 < 2 < 3 < 5

Answer: 1/8, 2/8, 3/8, 5/8 🎉

🐜🐝🦋🦅

Smallest → Biggest = Ascending Order  |  Biggest → Smallest = Descending Order
