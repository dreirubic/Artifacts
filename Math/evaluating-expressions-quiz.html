📘 Quick Lesson — How to Evaluate

Evaluating an algebraic expression means finding its value by replacing the variable(s) with given numbers, then computing the answer.

📌 Steps to Evaluate
  1. Write the expression.
  2. Substitute — replace the variable with the given value.
  3. Compute — follow the order of operations (×÷ before +−).
  4. Write your final answer.
Example 1 — One variable
5x + 3, if x = 2
= 5(2) + 3
= 10 + 3
= 13
Answer: 13
Example 2 — One variable
4n − 6, if n = 5
= 4(5) − 6
= 20 − 6
= 14
Answer: 14
Example 3 — Two variables
2a + 3b, if a=4, b=2
= 2(4) + 3(2)
= 8 + 6
= 14
Answer: 14
Example 4 — With exponent
x² + 1, if x = 3
= (3)² + 1
= 9 + 1
= 10
Answer: 10
⚠️ Remember: Always substitute first, then compute. Multiplication and division come before addition and subtraction!
