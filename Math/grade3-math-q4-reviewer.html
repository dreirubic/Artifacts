What is Multiplication?

▼

Multiplication is a faster way to add the same number many times.

3 + 3 + 3 + 3 = 12   →   3 × 4 = 12
We say: "3 times 4 equals 12"

The numbers you multiply are called factors. The answer is called the product.

6 × 7 = 42
Factors: 6 and 7   |   Product: 42
📋

Properties of Multiplication

▼

Commutative Property — You can change the order of factors; the product is still the same.

4 × 6 = 24   and   6 × 4 = 24 ✓

Zero Property — Any number multiplied by 0 equals 0.

9 × 0 = 0    100 × 0 = 0

Identity Property — Any number multiplied by 1 equals itself.

7 × 1 = 7    25 × 1 = 25
➗

What is Division?

▼

Division means sharing equally or grouping equally. It is the opposite of multiplication.

12 ÷ 4 = 3
"12 shared equally into 4 groups → 3 in each group"

Parts of division:

Dividend ÷ Divisor = Quotient
    12      ÷     4       =     3
Dividend = the number being divided
Divisor = the number you divide by
Quotient = the answer
🔗

Fact Families (Multiplication & Division)

▼

Multiplication and division are related! A fact family uses the same three numbers.

Numbers: 3, 5, 15
3 × 5 = 15    5 × 3 = 15
15 ÷ 3 = 5    15 ÷ 5 = 3
💡 If you know 8 × 7 = 56, then you also know: 56 ÷ 8 = 7 and 56 ÷ 7 = 8!
📌

Rules of Division

▼

Any number ÷ 1 = that same number.

9 ÷ 1 = 9

Any number ÷ itself = 1.

7 ÷ 7 = 1

0 ÷ any number = 0.

0 ÷ 5 = 0

You CANNOT divide by 0.

📖

Word Problem Strategies

▼

Use Polya's Steps to solve word problems:

1. UNDERSTAND — What is asked? What are the given facts?
2. PLAN — What operation will you use? (× or ÷)
3. SOLVE — Do the math!
4. CHECK — Does the answer make sense?

Clue words for Multiplication: each, every, in all, total, altogether, groups of, times

Clue words for Division: share equally, split, each person gets, how many groups, per

Example: Ana has 6 boxes. Each box has 8 crayons. How many crayons in all?
→ Multiply: 6 × 8 = 48 crayons
Example: 30 students are divided into 5 equal groups. How many students per group?
→ Divide: 30 ÷ 5 = 6 students
