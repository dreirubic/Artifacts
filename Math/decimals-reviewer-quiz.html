🔢 Part 1: Estimating Sums
What is estimating?
Estimating means finding a close but not exact answer. We round decimals to the nearest whole number first, then add.
💡 Rounding Rule:
• If the decimal part is .5 or more → round UP
• If the decimal part is less than .5 → round DOWN
🔍 How to Estimate a Sum:
1
Round each decimal to the nearest whole number.
2
Add the rounded numbers.
3
Use the ≈ (approximately equal) sign for estimates.
4
Use the = (equal) sign once you finish the math.
✏️ Example: Estimate 25.38 + 11.91

25.38 ≈ 25  (since .38 < .5, round down)
11.91 ≈ 12  (since .91 ≥ .5, round up)

25.38 + 11.91 ≈ 25 + 12 = 37

✅ The estimated value is 37.
✏️ Example: Estimate 32.9 + 8.99

32.9 ≈ 33  (since .9 ≥ .5, round up)
8.99 ≈ 9  (since .99 ≥ .5, round up)

32.9 + 8.99 ≈ 33 + 9 = 42
➕ Part 2: Adding Decimals
Adding decimals is just like adding whole numbers — but you need to line up the decimal points!
🔍 Steps to Add Decimals:
1
Write the numbers in a column. Line up the decimal points.
2
Add zeros if needed so both numbers have the same decimal places.
3
Add from right to left. Don't forget to carry when needed!
4
Put the decimal point in your answer.
5
Check: Is your answer close to your estimate? ✅
✏️ Example: Find 25.38 + 11.91

  1
 2 5 . 3 8
+ 1 1 . 9 1
───────────
 3 7 . 2 9

✅ 25.38 + 11.91 = 37.29
Close to our estimate of 37 → Reasonable! 👍
✏️ Example: Find 32.9 + 8.99

  1 1
 3 2 . 9 0
+  8 . 9 9
───────────
 4 1 . 8 9

✅ 32.9 + 8.99 = 41.89
💡 Remember: When you add a whole number with decimals (like 4.4 + 5.63), write it as 4.40 + 5.63 — just add a zero at the end!
📝 Part 3: Solving Word Problems
For word problems, always follow these steps:
1
Understand – What is being asked?
2
Plan – What operation will you use? (add or subtract?)
3
Solve – Write and compute your number sentence.
4
Check – Does your answer make sense?
✏️ Problem 1 (Addition):
Sarah bought 5.42 m of lace. Anna bought 3.26 m of lace. How many meters did they buy in all?

Operation: Addition (in all = add)
Number sentence: 5.42 + 3.26 = ?

 5 . 4 2
+ 3 . 2 6
─────────
 8 . 6 8

✅ They bought 8.68 meters of lace in all.
✏️ Problem 2 (Multi-step / More than one item):
Jahli spent ₱26.50 on milk and ₱22.15 on a snack. How much did he spend altogether?

Operation: Addition
Number sentence: ₱26.50 + ₱22.15 = ?

 2 6 . 5 0
+ 2 2 . 1 5
──────────
 4 8 . 6 5

✅ Jahli spent ₱48.65 altogether.
💡 Key words to help you:
• Add → in all, altogether, total, both, combined, sum
• Subtract → less, fewer, how much more, difference, left, remain
