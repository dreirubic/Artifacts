🪵 Woodworking — Tools & Materials

📌 What is Woodworking?

Woodworking is the skill of cutting, shaping, and joining wood to make useful objects like furniture, cabinets, and decorations.

🪚 Cutting Tools

🪚
Hand SawCuts wood into pieces using sharp teeth.
🔪
Crosscut SawCuts wood across the grain.
🔪
Rip SawCuts wood along the grain.
🗡️
ChiselShapes or removes small bits of wood. Used with a mallet.

✈️ Shaping & Measuring Tools

✈️
Hand PlaneSmooths and flattens wood surfaces.
📄
SandpaperSmooths rough surfaces by rubbing.
🔨
Claw HammerDrives and removes nails.
🔧
ScrewdriverTurns screws to join wood pieces.
📐
Try SquareChecks if corners are exactly 90°.
〰️
Marking GaugeMarks a line parallel to an edge.
🎨 Wood Finishing Materials

🖌️ What is Wood Finishing?

Wood finishing means applying materials to the surface of wood to protect it from moisture and damage — and to make it look beautiful!

🧴 Finishing Materials

🎨
PaintAdds color and protects wood from moisture and wear.
✨
VarnishClear/tinted coating that hardens and adds shine to surfaces.
🛢️
Wood StainAdds color while showing the wood's natural grain.
🧴
LacquerFast-drying clear finish for a smooth, glossy surface.
🪵
Wood PuttyPaste that fills cracks and holes in wood before painting.
🟫
Asphalt Wood SealerDark asphalt-based primer that seals and waterproofs wood.
🪟
VeneerA very thin decorative sheet of wood glued onto furniture for an attractive finish.
🧱
FRC BoardFiber Reinforced Cement board — strong flat board used as a surface or backing in projects.
🤝 Adhesives (Glues & Binders)

🤝 What are Adhesives?

Adhesives are sticky substances used to bond or join two pieces together without nails or screws.

🧴 Types of Adhesives

🟡
Wood Glue (PVA)Most common woodworking adhesive — strong when dry.
🔵
Contact CementApplied to BOTH surfaces, then pressed together for a strong instant bond.
🪝
EpoxyTwo parts mixed together — very strong, bonds wood, metal & more.
🔫
Hot GlueMelted glue from a glue gun — fast but less durable.
⚙️ Woodworking Processes

📋 5 Major Woodworking Processes

1
MillingCutting and shaping raw lumber into flat, usable boards. It prepares wood before any other work begins.
2
SawingUsing a saw to cut wood into pieces of the desired shape or size. Usually the first hands-on step.
3
Joining & PlaningJoining connects two wood pieces together. Planing smooths and flattens surfaces using a hand plane.
4
Drilling & BoringMaking round holes in wood using a drill or brace-and-bit. Boring makes larger holes. Done before inserting screws or bolts.
5
RoutingCutting decorative edges, grooves, or patterns into wood using a router tool.
💡Remember! Measure twice, cut once! Keep all tools clean and stored safely after use.
🔧 Metalworking — Tools & Materials

📌 What is Metalworking?

Metalworking is the process of cutting, shaping, and joining metal to create objects like tools, pipes, and machine parts. Metal is harder than wood so it needs special tools.

✂️ Tools in Metalworking

✂️
HacksawCuts metal pipes, rods, and bars with a fine-toothed blade.
✂️
Tin Snips / ShearsCuts thin sheet metal like scissors.
🔥
Cold ChiselCuts or chips metal when hit with a hammer.
📋
Metal FileSmooths and shapes metal surfaces.
🔩
Bench ViseHolds metal firmly while you work on it.
🔨
Ball-Peen HammerShapes and flattens metal.
🔧
PliersGrips, bends, and cuts wire.
🔑
WrenchTightens or loosens nuts and bolts.
🕳️
Electric DrillMakes holes in metal with metal drill bits.
🔩
Rivet GunJoins metal sheets using small metal pins called rivets.

⚙️ Materials in Metalworking

🔩
SteelStrong and common — structures and tools.
🥄
AluminumLight and rust-resistant.
🪙
CopperUsed in pipes and electrical wiring.
📦
Tin / Sheet MetalThin flat metal for cans and roofing.
🧪
Welding RodMelts to join two pieces of metal.
⚙️ Metalworking Processes

📋 5 Major Metalworking Processes

1
Cutting Pieces of MetalUsing tools like a hacksaw, tin snips, or cold chisel to cut metal into the desired shape or size. Always the first step.
2
WeldingJoining two metal pieces by melting them together using extreme heat. A welding rod melts and fuses the pieces into one strong joint.
3
CastingPouring melted (liquid) metal into a mold so it cools and hardens into a specific shape. Used for engine parts and cookware.
4
MoldingShaping metal by pressing it into or around a mold. Used for forming sheets or tubes.
5
JoiningConnecting metal pieces using bolts, rivets, screws, or welding to make the finished product strong and durable.
⚠️Safety First! Always wear gloves and goggles when working with metal. Edges are sharp and welding sparks can burn!
🎋 Bamboo Arts & Crafts — Tools & Materials

📌 What is Bamboo Craft?

Bamboo craft is the art of making useful and beautiful objects using bamboo. It is common in the Philippines, eco-friendly, and uses bamboo's strength and flexibility!

🔪 Tools Used in Bamboo Crafts

🔪
Bolo / MacheteCuts and splits bamboo poles into strips.
🪚
HandsawCuts bamboo poles to the right length.
🗡️
Knife / Utility KnifeTrims and cleans bamboo strips.
📋
ScraperSmooths strips to prevent splintering.
🧵
Weaving NeedleThreads strips over and under while weaving.
🗜️
Clamp / RopeHolds bamboo in place while drying.

🎋 Materials in Bamboo Crafts

🎋
Bamboo CulmThe main bamboo pole — used for furniture and frames.
〰️
Bamboo StripsThin flat pieces split from bamboo — used for weaving.
🧵
Rattan / Abaca TwineNatural cord for tying and binding bamboo.
🧴
Lacquer / VarnishProtects finished bamboo products.
🎨
Natural DyesColors bamboo strips for decorative designs.
🧺 Bamboo Arts & Crafts Processes

📋 5 Major Bamboo Craft Processes

1
SplittingUsing a bolo or knife to split the bamboo pole lengthwise into flat strips. This is always the FIRST step before any crafting.
2
ScrapingSmoothing bamboo strips with a scraper to remove rough edges and splinters — makes them safe and easy to weave.
3
DyeingColoring bamboo strips using natural or synthetic dyes before weaving to add decorative patterns and colors.
4
WeavingInterlacing bamboo strips over and under each other to form mats, baskets, walls, and other woven products. The most important skill!
5
Panel MakingArranging and fastening bamboo strips or poles side-by-side to create flat panels used for walls, floors, and furniture.

🧺 Common Bamboo Products

BasketsBayong (bags)Hats & FansFurnitureBangkito (stools)Wall PanelsPicture FramesWind Chimes
🌿Fun Fact! Bamboo is called the "poor man's timber" because it grows fast, costs less, and can replace expensive wood in many projects!
