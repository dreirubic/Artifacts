What is a Pronoun?

A pronoun is a word we use instead of a noun (a person, place, or thing) so we don't have to repeat the same name over and over again.

Without a pronoun:
Maria went to school. Maria brought Maria's bag. Maria loves school.

With pronouns:
Maria went to school. She brought her bag. She loves school.
💡 Pronouns make sentences shorter and easier to read! They're like shortcuts for names.
👤

Personal Pronouns

Personal pronouns tell us who is doing something (subject) or who receives the action (object).

Person Subject Pronoun
(doing)		 Object Pronoun
(receiving)		 Example
1st – Me I me I love books. Give it to me.
1st – Us we us We played. They helped us.
2nd – You you you You are kind. I like you.
3rd – Boy/Man he him He ran fast. I saw him.
3rd – Girl/Woman she her She sings. I called her.
3rd – Thing/Animal it it It is cute. I fed it.
3rd – Many they them They danced. I love them.
🏠

Possessive Pronouns

Possessive pronouns tell us who owns something. There are two kinds!

Person Possessive Adjective
(before a noun)		 Possessive Pronoun
(stands alone)		 Example
I / Me my mine This is my bag. That bag is mine.
We / Us our ours This is our room. It is ours.
You your yours Is this your pencil? It is yours.
He / Him his his That is his book. The book is his.
She / Her her hers I like her drawing. It is hers.
It its — The dog wagged its tail.
They / Them their theirs I love their house. The house is theirs.
🧠
Easy trick! Ask yourself:
▸ "Who is doing it?" → use a subject pronoun (I, she, he, we, they…)
▸ "Who does it happen to?" → use an object pronoun (me, her, him, us, them…)
▸ "Who owns it?" → use a possessive pronoun (my/mine, her/hers…)
