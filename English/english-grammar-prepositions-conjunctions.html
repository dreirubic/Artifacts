Lesson 1
📍 Prepositions of Place
A preposition of place tells us WHERE something or someone is. It gives us the location! 📌
PrepositionMeaningExample Sentence
ininside somethingThe cat is in the box.
onon top of a surfaceThe book is on the table.
underbelow somethingThe dog is under the chair.
behindat the back ofThe ball is behind the door.
in front ofat the front ofShe stands in front of the class.
beside / next toat the side ofHe sits beside his friend.
betweenin the middle of two thingsThe park is between two buildings.
abovehigher than somethingThe bird flies above the tree.
⭐ Tip: Ask yourself — "WHERE is it?" — and use a preposition of place to answer! 📍
Lesson 2
⏰ Prepositions of Time
A preposition of time tells us WHEN something happens. It gives us the time! 🕐
PrepositionUsed for…Example Sentence
atspecific time (hours)School starts at 7:30 AM.
inmonths, years, seasons, parts of dayWe play in the afternoon.
onspecific days and datesWe go to church on Sunday.
beforeearlier thanBrush your teeth before sleeping.
afterlater thanWe eat dessert after dinner.
duringwhile something is happeningI napped during the vacation.
⭐ Easy Rule:
Use AT for time (at 3 PM) · Use ON for days (on Monday) · Use IN for months/seasons (in December, in summer) 🕐
Lesson 3
🔗 Coordinating Conjunctions
A conjunction is a word that connects two ideas, words, or sentences together. Think of it as a bridge! 🌉
AND
adds ideas together
I like cats and dogs.
She reads and draws.
OR
shows a choice
Do you want juice or water?
We can walk or ride a bike.
BUT
shows a difference
I like apples but not oranges.
She is tall but he is short.
SO
shows a result
It was raining, so I used an umbrella.
She was tired, so she slept early.
⭐ Memory Trick: Think of BOAS — But, Or, And, So!
AND = adds · OR = choice · BUT = difference · SO = result 🔗
