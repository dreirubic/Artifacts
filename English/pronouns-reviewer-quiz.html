🧠 What are Pronouns?

Pronouns are words that take the place of nouns (names of people, places, or things). Instead of saying a name over and over again, we use a pronoun!

Example:
❌ "Maria went to the park. Maria played there all day."
✅ "Maria went to the park. She played there all day."
👤 Personal Pronouns Pages 274–275

Personal pronouns are used as simple substitutes for the names of persons.

I you he she it we they him her us them

📌 When do we use each pronoun?

🔴 He / Him — for a boy or man
   → Mr. Gonzales teaches Math. He teaches in a public school.
🩷 She / Her — for a girl or woman
   → Aling Andrea went to the market. She bought fish and vegetables.
🟢 It — for a thing, animal, or place
   → Mr. Santos bought a new car. It is colored white.
🔵 We / Us — for me + other people
   → My classmate and I are going on a picnic. We are going on a picnic.
🟣 They / Them — for more than one person or thing
   → Cristy and Trishia are cleaning their room. They are cleaning their room.
🟠 You — when talking directly to someone
   → Come here, Erin. You better be ready for school.
🔴 I — when talking about yourself
   → I am a Bulakeño. I speak Tagalog and English.
🏷️ Possessive Pronouns Page 285

Possessive pronouns are special pronouns that show ownership — who something belongs to!

Pronoun Possessive Form Example
ImyMy new dress is a gift.
youyourYou look good in your pink blouse.
hehisRaul takes care of his puppy.
sheherRona brought her new umbrella.
ititsThe dog wagged its tail.
weourOur house is small but clean.
theytheirThe boys submitted their projects.
💡 Tip: Ask yourself — "Who owns it?" Then pick the right possessive pronoun!

→ Janice and Precious kept (our, their, your) own money in the bank.
(Janice and Precious = they → possessive = their ✅)
⚡ Quick Cheat Sheet
1 person = he / she / it
Many people = they
Me + others = we
Talking to someone = you
Thing / animal / place = it
About yourself = I
